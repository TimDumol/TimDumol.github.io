About me

Hey there. I'm Tim, and I love solving problems using code, math, and algorithms. I am experienced in site reliability engineering, data engineering, algorithms and programming contests, and web development. My mathematical and algorithms background means that I am comfortable with machine learning, optimization and statistics.

I consider myself performance- and security-focused, yet pragmatic in my design choices. I attempt to solve problems rather than blindly follow specifications -- I consider problems within their context and look for more efficient solutions. I am flexible with my technology choices, and consider myself a polyglot, although I have a preference for statically or optionally typed languages. I've also helped organize some algorithmic contests myself to show people the fun challenges you can tackle in computer science.

Code

  • rust-otp
    A Rust library for performing the HMAC-based One-Time Password (HOTP) and Time-based One-Time Password (TOTP) algorithms, which are the algorithms used in many 2FA apps, such as Google Authenticator.
  • unix-crypt-td-js
    An implementation of the Unix crypt(3) hash in Javascript.
  • guard-stylus
    A Guard plugin to watch and compile Stylus files.
  • guard-jade
    A Guard plugin to watch and compile Jade files.
  • guard-copy2
    A Guard plugin to automatically copy files from a source directory to a target directory.
  • angular-momentum
    Scaffold for new projects using Vagrant, Flask, SQLAlchemy, Puppet, Coffeescript, AngularJS, Stylus, and Jade. Originally made for the MIT AITI Philippines 2013 class.
  • TrueSwift
    A TaskRabbit-alike, and my team's submission to AngelHack Manila 2013. Was one of the 12 finalists.

I've also contributed small patches and documentation fixes to various open source projects, including Vagrant, Sage Notebook, and Rust.
